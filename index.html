Southern Africa / Republic of Namibia / Land of the Brave
Explore Africa's bright treasure that has 300 sunny days a year! From the Namib Desert in the north, to the Atlantic Ocean and the Namib-Naukluft National Park in the West, the Republic of Namibia has the world's oldest natural beauties that can satisfy all tastes.

Namibia, The Land of the Brave

Unity, Liberty, Justice

Namibia, officially known as the Republic of Namibia, is a country in Southern Africa. The name of the country is derived from the word "Namib", which means "vast place". It is situated between Namib and Kalahari deserts and its western border is the Atlantic Ocean! It shares borders with many African countries like Zambia and Angola to the north, Botswana to the east and South Africa to the south and east. The climate is overall arid and droughts are common, however, near the coastal area there are low temperatures and thick fogs. The country is home to diverse wildlife that is protected in 12 national parks. The capital is Windhoek. It is located in central Namibia and is its largest city. Windhoek is the social, economic and cultural centre of the Namibia and is the home of many enterprises, educational and cultural institutions and historical sites.

Windhoek

Windhoek is the center of Namibia - a busy metropolis with a fast-paced lifestyle but not as overwhelming and hectic as the other African cities. It is a clean, orderly and well-organized city influenced by both African and European cultures which is evident not only in the architecture that includes both modern and German-colonial buildings, but in the local cuisine and lifestyle as well. There are many historical buildings like Alte Feste or the Christkirche, museums like the Independence Memorial Museum and galleries like the National Art Gallery of Namibia. Visitors can rest in many green areas and parks around the city like the National Botanical Gardens, the Zoo Park or the Daan Viljoen Game Park.
